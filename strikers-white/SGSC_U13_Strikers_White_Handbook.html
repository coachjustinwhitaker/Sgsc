A regular email covering the concepts we're working on: chapters to read, videos to watch, exercises to try. Show up to training having already started thinking.

Team Platform TBD

We'll confirm our team platform before the season starts. It will serve as the home base for additional information, games to watch, training opportunities, and general team communication.

Text Message Group

Used sparingly for time-sensitive information: weather changes, game cancellations, last-minute updates. If you get a text from the group, it matters.

Playing Time

Every player who shows up deserves to play, and they will. Starts and minutes are decided through what we see in training and what the game situation calls for. The best thing a player can do to earn more time on the field is show up consistently, work hard, and be ready when their number is called.

If Something's Wrong

These things come up. If something feels off with your player, with the team dynamic, or with a decision we made, please reach out directly to Coach Justin. We'd rather have an honest conversation than have something fester. We ask that players and parents give it 24 hours after a game before reaching out, so everyone has time to decompress.

What to Expect From Us

We'll communicate consistently and clearly throughout the season. Strikers Weekly keeps everyone oriented week to week. Striker Study keeps players growing between sessions. The team platform is where deeper resources live. Texts are reserved for when something needs to be known right away. This handbook is a living document: when something changes, this page gets updated. The last updated date at the top reflects the current version.

Team Life & Expectations

Player Responsibilities

Being part of the Strikers White means showing up: not just physically, but mentally and emotionally. We don't expect perfection. We expect commitment.

  • Come Prepared Cleats, shin guards, a properly inflated ball, and plenty to drink. Bring something to refuel on too: a bar, some fruit, or anything that helps you sustain energy through a full session. Training demands a lot physically: players who take care of their bodies show up sharper and last longer. Arrive early and ready to go.
  • Stay Engaged Listen during instruction, ask questions, and keep distractions to a minimum. Your focus and energy affect the whole team.
  • Train with Discipline Discipline means doing what needs to be done, even when it's hard or unnoticed. Holding yourself to a high standard is a form of respect.
  • Work Hard Consistently Games aren't won on game day: they're earned in training. How we train is how we play.
  • Support the Team Celebrate others' success, stay positive, and be the kind of teammate you'd want to play with.
  • Embrace Growth Mistakes are not failures: they're chances to learn. Reflect, adapt, and take ownership of your journey.
Striker Study
Striker Study

Our regular learning series, sent to players and parents. Each edition brings something to read, watch, or work on before the next session: a Soccer IQ chapter, a footwork sequence, a tactical breakdown, a game to watch. The goal is simple: show up to training having already started thinking.

Game Day Expectations

Arrival & Warm-Up

  • Arrive 45 minutes before kickoff
  • Warm-up starts 40 minutes before game time
  • Dynamic movement, rondos, team keep-away
  • Quick focused ball movement drills

What to Bring

  • Cleats and shin guards
  • Water or a sports drink
  • Something to refuel at halftime: a bar, fruit, or a light snack
  • Full uniform: home and away tops, shorts, socks

On the Bench

  • The bench is part of the team, so stay focused and ready
  • Know your positional rotation
  • Bring energy and communication as soon as you step on

After the Whistle

  • Shake hands with opponents and referees
  • Regroup for a quick post-game huddle
  • Parents are encouraged to join
  • Focus on what we learned and how we grew, not just the score
Sideline Behavior

This section is for parents and supporters. The sideline is a powerful place, and we ask everyone on it to help make it one of encouragement and growth. When parents and coaches are aligned, players feel the difference: they take risks, make decisions, and learn through experience without hesitation.

1

Encourage, Don't Instruct

Please avoid coaching from the sideline. Our players are developing decision-making and technique. What may look wrong in the moment may be exactly what they're learning to explore. Cheer effort. Celebrate courage. Let the game teach.

2

Respect the Referees

Referees are human, and many are learning, just like our players. NorCal Premier has made referee respect a cornerstone of its mission, and we support that fully. No yelling at referees. No questioning calls. Coaches handle any issues through appropriate channels.

3

Model Sportsmanship

Treat the opposing team and coaches with the same respect we expect for our own. Applaud good soccer on both sides. Your calm presence becomes an anchor for the team when emotions run high.

4

Trust the Process

You might not always see the reasoning behind every decision in the moment, and that's okay. A player may be working on composure, positioning, or a specific role. What looks like the wrong choice is sometimes exactly what we asked them to try. Your patience and trust make that kind of learning possible.

5

Be the Boost

Our players hear you when you clap, when you say their name, and when you show up. Be the voice that lifts them up when it's hard. Make the sideline a space of strength.

Foundations of the Team

Why We Coach

We coach because we believe in what this game gives back when it's taught with care. Not just technical skills, but the confidence that comes from solving a problem under pressure, the character that comes from being held to a standard, and the connection that comes from being part of something bigger than yourself. We ask for bravery and expect to see mistakes. We want players who can read the game, make decisions, and find joy in the process of getting better. We're here because we love this game, and because we believe in what these players can become.

Smart · Brave · Connected
Team Values

Our values are more than words. They show up in how we respond to challenges, how we celebrate growth, and how we support each other.

Growth
Every mistake is a chance to learn. We train with curiosity, reflect with honesty, and keep striving to improve: not just in skills, but in how we think, communicate, and support others.
Effort
We give our best in every moment, whether playing, practicing, or cheering on teammates. Hustle, focus, and consistency matter more than perfection. Hard work is the foundation of confidence.
Teamwork
We succeed together. We support each other on and off the field, communicate with respect, and value every player's contribution. Being a great teammate means being present, positive, and ready to help others grow.
Respect
We show respect to coaches, referees, opponents, teammates, and ourselves. That means listening when someone is speaking, honoring the game, and playing with integrity, even when no one is watching.

How We Play

Our Game Model

A game model is a shared understanding of how we want to play: not just formations, but identity, behavior, and how we respond in every moment of the game.

For players, this means every training session has a direction: decisions are guided by shared principles, not guesswork. For parents, it means when you watch your player on the field, there's intention behind what you're seeing. These three words are the lens everything runs through.

Smart:

We read the game, understand our opponents, and know each other's strengths. We adapt, find space, and always support each other.

Brave:

We try things. We make decisions without hesitation and learn from what doesn't work. Mistakes are data, not failures.

Connected:

We play with intent. Methodical, patient, and in sync. We know when and how to strike together.

Player Model: Who We're Developing
Intelligent

Thinkers of the game. They anticipate, adapt, stay aware, and help teammates solve problems on the field.

Confident

Quiet strength. Comfortable on the ball, composed under pressure, willing to lead by example.

Skillful

Move with purpose. Control in tight spaces, strong positioning, expressive and effective.

Hardworking

Show up with heart. Tenacious in defense, relentless in attack, resilient when things don't go their way.

This model isn't about perfection: it's about pursuit. Every player on this team has the ability to grow into these traits, and every session is a chance to take one more step forward.

Practice Structure

Every session follows the same four-part rhythm, and that consistency is intentional. When players already know the shape of a session, they spend less energy figuring out what comes next and more energy on the actual learning. The structure becomes the container, and what happens inside it gets sharper every week. The closing reflection isn't a cooldown: it's the moment that connects what the body just did to what the brain needs to remember.

1

Activation — Prepare the Body and Mind

  • Opening huddle
  • Dynamic movement series
  • Rondos: scanning, rhythm, decision-making
  • Small-sided games to wake up the brain
2

Skill Building — Technical & Tactical Growth

  • Individual ball work and partner activities
  • First touch, passing, dribbling, finishing
  • Increasing pressure and decision-making scenarios
3

Team Play — Learning in Context

  • Pattern play and formations
  • Position-specific scenarios
  • Movement, spacing, and transitions
  • Scrimmages
4

Reflection — Connecting Awareness

  • Closing huddle
  • Setting expectations for next session
