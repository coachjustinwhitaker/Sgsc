4v3  ·  7 min
Build Out Under Press
Out Loud
  • Attackers, can you find the free player? The numbers are in your favor.
  • Defenders, are you pressing as a unit? Who is triggering the press?
Observe — 4v3
Cognition
  • Who finds the free player in the numbers-up situation
  • Who triggers and organizes the press without being asked
  • Patience in possession — who waits for the right moment vs forces it?
Competence
  • Passing weight and accuracy under press pressure
  • Defensive shape and press execution as a unit
Character
  • Who organizes and communicates without being asked
  • Response when possession is lost — immediate press or watching?
6v4  ·  7 min
Shape and Triangles
Out Loud
  • Attackers, what does your shape look like? Are you flat or creating triangles and diamonds?
  • Defenders, can you stay compact? Are you covering the passing lanes?
Observe — 6v4  ·  Watch Wingbacks
Cognition
  • Do attackers understand how their shape creates or closes passing lanes?
  • Who adjusts position to create triangles and diamonds without being told
  • Defensive compactness — who covers lanes vs who ball watches?
Competence
  • Passing quality into tight spaces
  • Wingbacks driving, combining, and getting back into shape
Character
  • Who maintains concentration and shape under increased pressure
  • Communication between attackers about shape and positioning
6v6  ·  6 min
Equal Numbers — Free Play
Out Loud
  • Can you maintain your shape without the numbers advantage?
  • Are you still pressing as a unit or going individually?
Observe — 6v6
Cognition
  • Do they maintain shape or revert to individual play when numbers equalize?
  • Off-ball movement — constantly adjusting relative to ball, teammates, defenders?
  • Who makes the right decision without a structural advantage to lean on?
Competence
  • Technical execution under equal pressure
  • Shape maintenance and combination play without numbers advantage
Character
  • Who leads and organizes when the game gets harder
  • Who maintains effort and communication in the final progression
Station B
Continuous 2v2 with GKs
Two Aligned Goals with GKs — Continuous Transition, No Stoppages

Run It

  • Two players from Team A attack Team B’s goal
  • The moment Team A shoots or loses the ball, two players from Team B release and attack Team A’s goal — Team A immediately transitions to defend
  • Continuous, no stoppages, self-perpetuating
  • GK can be used as a passing option on the attack
  • Coach manages the release line and keeps the pace up
  • Group 2 runs Station B first — Group 1 rotates in after swap
Watch For
Cognition
  • Transition recognition — already moving before the shot lands or watching first?
  • Decision making in the 2v2 — drive solo, combine with partner, or use the GK?
  • GK distribution speed and decision to launch the next attack
Competence
  • Recovery effort and angle — full sprint or half effort?
  • Defensive positioning and recovery mechanics
  • Finishing quality under pressure
Character
  • Effort level as fatigue builds across 20 minutes
  • Transition effort — competing both ways at the same intensity?
  • Response when the transition fails — keep competing or switch off?
