Block 2
Dynamic Warmup + Passing Series
All players  ·  Turf surface — push sharper cuts  ·  Feints emphasized tonight  ·  20 min
Format
All players
Time
0:05–0:25
Duration
20 min
Equipment
Balls, cones
Mirrored Dynamic Warmup — Parallel Lines with Center Cones

Run It

  • Coaches lead mirrored dynamic warmup
  • Progress into passing series
  • Turf surface allows sharper cuts and quicker directional changes — push players to commit to moves they might not attempt on grass
  • Final sequence: dribble, feints, directional change, then pass
  • Keep it moving — no standing
Out Loud
  • Can you feel the difference in the surface tonight? How does that change what you can do with your feet?
  • What does your first touch set up? Where are you going?
  • Can you be sharper tonight? What does sharp look like?
  • Can you pass with intent?
Observe
Cognition

  • Communication with passing partner — has it improved?
  • Who self-adjusts movement based on the surface without being told
Competence
  • Feint quality and commitment on turf
  • First touch direction and weight
  • Technical consistency across the full sequence
Character
  • Who pushes their own ceiling in warmup vs who stays comfortable
  • Energy level — bringing it from the first minute?
  • Who self-corrects without being told

Station 1
Shoot and Chase to Defend
Two Lines — Shot Triggers Simultaneous Release — First Attacker Immediately Recovers to Defend

Run It

  • Two lines of attackers with balls, one line per goal (full-size and offset pop-up)
  • First attacker from line one drives at the full-size goal and shoots
  • Second attacker in line two is held until the shot is taken, then immediately released to drive at the offset goal
  • First attacker must transition and sprint to recover as a defender the instant they shoot — the shot releases everything
  • Coach or server on the side manages the hold and release of line two
  • No standing. The moment a shot is taken the next sequence starts.
Out Loud
  • Are you already turning? The shot is not the end of your rep.
  • Can you get there? Show me the sprint.
  • Defender, can you slow them down? Take away the easy option.
Intervene
  • Stop a player who watches their shot before transitioning. "When does your defensive rep start? It starts when the ball leaves your foot."
  • Stop a player who jogs the recovery sprint. "Is that the effort you would give in a game? Show me what a real recovery sprint looks like."
Observe
Cognition
  • Transition recognition speed — turning before the shot lands or watching the outcome first?
  • Recovery angle — taking the right line to cut off the attacker at the offset goal?
  • Does the incoming attacker read the recovering defender and adjust their approach?
Competence
  • Defensive body position and footwork on recovery
  • Ability to slow the attacker and take away the easy option when fatigued
  • Shot quality is secondary here
Character
  • How fast do they mentally switch from attacker to defender?
  • Recovery sprint effort after already shooting — full sprint or jogging?
  • Competitive intensity when already gassed
  • Response when they fail to recover in time — keep competing or switch off?
Evaluation Priority  Character Read

The shooting is the trigger, not the evaluation. You care about what happens the instant the ball leaves their foot. A player already sprinting to defend before their shot lands is showing you something. A player who watches their shot and then jogs is telling you something equally clear.

Station 2
1v1 with Recovering CB
Attacker on Offsides Angle — CB Starts Disadvantaged — GK Organizes Throughout

Run It

  • Attacker runs an offside angle, receives ball and drives at goal
  • CB starts from a disadvantaged recovery position — behind the attacker or offset
  • CB must sprint to recover and defend before the attacker can shoot
  • GK organizes and commands their box throughout
  • Rotate attacker and CB roles each rep — keep transitions fast
Out Loud
  • CB, are you sprinting to recover? Can you still make this play?
  • Attacker, can you stay composed? You have the advantage.
  • GK, are you organizing your defender? Can they hear you?
Intervene
  • Stop a CB who gives up the recovery. "Is that rep over? Show me you can still make this play."
  • Stop a GK who goes quiet. "Can your defender hear you? What are you telling them?"
Observe
Cognition
  • Attacker decision making with time and space — rushing or staying composed?
  • CB recovery angle — taking the right line to cut off the attacker?
  • GK communication — directing the CB to the right position?
  • Does the attacker read the CB recovery and adjust their approach?
Competence
  • Attacker finishing quality under pressure
  • CB sprint effort and recovery mechanics
  • GK positioning, handling, and distribution after a save
Character
  • CB effort when already behind — full sprint or half effort?
  • Attacker composure when the CB is closing fast
  • GK leadership and communication under pressure
  • How both players respond when the rep does not go their way
Evaluation Priority  Effort Filter

The CB recovery is your effort filter at this station. A defender who sprints hard when already beaten is showing you Character that goes beyond defending. GK communication and organization is a bonus Cognition and Character read.

Station 3
JukeBox
6–8 x 12 Grid — Two Inset Gates Each Side — 1v1, Teammates Tag In Continuously
Scoring
1
Controlled dribble through a gate
1
Juke
1
Nutmeg
2
Combo — any combination move

Run It

  • 1v1 inside the grid — play to 3 points, then both players tag out and two new teammates step in
  • Rounds end at 3 points — the player who reaches 3 tags out immediately, their opponent tags out too, next two in
  • This creates different matchups each round and more chaos than a line — that is the point
  • Player must control first touch before making moves
  • Coaches make sure every player is getting tagged in — intervene if someone is being skipped
  • Call scoring out loud — create the energy
  • Music on, high energy, coaches are loud and celebratory
Out Loud — Call It
  • ONE POINT. NUTMEG. TWO POINTS COMBO.
  • Are you showing us something? This is your stage.
  • Can you go again? Same energy?
Intervene — One Rep Per Pool Rotation
  • Deliver specific feedback on body shape or first touch. Watch the immediate response before they step back in.
  • What were you trying there? Can you show me that again with your whole body committed?
Observe
Cognition
  • Who reads their opponent quickly and adjusts their approach
  • Who organizes the rotation without being asked
Competence
  • Creativity and ambition — trying moves or playing safe even here?
  • First touch quality before making moves
  • Technical execution of jukes, nutmegs, and combinations under fatigue
Character
  • Who thrives in the performance environment vs who tightens up
  • Response to getting beaten — reset immediately or carry it?
  • Sideline players — engaged and encouraging or mentally elsewhere?
  • Applies the coaching intervention immediately on the next rep?
  • Same intensity on rep ten as rep one?
Evaluation Priority  Coachability Read

JukeBox is your coachability read in a high-energy context. The intervention moment here is your clearest window into how a player receives and applies feedback — the environment is loose and the stakes feel low. A player who thrives, then receives feedback and applies it immediately, is showing you Silver Character. A player who tightens up in JukeBox is showing you something too.

Block 4
Scrimmage
Three-team round robin  ·  U14 as third team if available  ·  Open play  ·  No interventions
Format
3-team round robin
Rotation
Losing team off
Rounds
10 min rounds
Time
1:25–1:55

Run It

  • Three teams — losing team rotates off, winning team stays on, next team comes on
  • Open play, no constraints
  • If U14 team joins, fold them in as the third team
  • Coaches observe — no interventions
  • If stations ran short, extend scrimmage time accordingly
Observe — Fatigue Strips Everything Artificial Away
Cognition
  • Decision making quality when legs are gone — simplifies intelligently or falls apart?
  • Who is still scanning and positioning when fatigued
  • How U13 players adapt tactically against older players
Competence
  • Technical quality under full fatigue — what holds up and what disappears?
  • Confirm reads, do not build new ones
Character
  • Effort level when fatigued — who keeps competing vs who coasts?
  • Communication under fatigue — who keeps talking vs who goes silent?
  • Who organizes and drives the group when everyone is tired
  • Response to competing against U14s — who rises vs who shrinks?
  • Frustration, blaming teammates vs composure and accountability
Evaluation Priority  Silver Disqualifier

Fatigue strips everything artificial away. You are seeing the real player now. Negativity toward teammates under fatigue is a Silver disqualifier regardless of technical ability. Watch specifically for who competes harder when the opposition steps up.
