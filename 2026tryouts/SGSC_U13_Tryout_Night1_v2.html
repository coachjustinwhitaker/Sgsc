Block 3
Rondos 4v1
Groups of 6  ·  4 outside, 1 defending, 1 recovery  ·  Self-managing — coaches rove
Groups
6 players
Format
4 outside, 1 def, 1 recovery
Time
0:20–0:35
Duration
15 min
4v1 Rondo — Player Who Loses Ball Goes to Middle, Recovery Player Rotates In

Run It

  • 4 outside players keep possession from 1 defender
  • 6th player in passive recovery — rotates in when defender wins or player loses ball
  • Player who loses the ball goes to the middle
  • Self-managing — coaches rove with extra balls, do not run it
Out Loud
  • Where are the angles? Do you have more than one option?
  • Can we create space after the pass?
  • Can we tighten this up? Do we need multiple touches?
  • Are we communicating?
Intervene
  • You passed it. Now where are you going? Is your job done?
  • Are you ready to receive the ball?
Observe
Cognition
  • Support movement after passing — repositioning or standing?
  • Decision speed under light pressure
  • Who organizes the rondo without being asked
  • Recovery player engagement — watching analytically or checked out?
Competence
  • Pass weight and accuracy under pressure
  • First touch quality in tight space
  • Body shape before receiving
Character
  • Body language in the middle after getting beaten
  • Resilience and effort when losing repeatedly in front of peers
  • Who encourages teammates vs who goes quiet
Block 4
Battle Boxes 1v1 to 2v1
Same groups of 6  ·  Two active, four rotating  ·  Coaches call matchups — players do not self-select
Format
Groups of 6
Box size
~10×15 yards
Time
0:35–0:50
Duration
15 min
Battle Box — Defending Player Passes Diagonally to Start, Attacker Crosses End Line to Score
Structure
Phase 1  ·  7–8 min 1v1. Defending player starts with the ball and passes it diagonally across the box to the attacker. Attacker touches and moves to cross one of the two end lines by dribbling. If attacker successfully crosses they return to the line. If they are defended they become the defender. Keep transitions fast.
Phase 2  ·  7–8 min 2v1. Extend the length of the box to double. Defending player starts with the ball at the back and passes to either attacker. Attackers work together to cross the box. Rotate defender role.
Out Loud
  • What is your first touch setting up? Where are you going with it?
  • Attackers — where is your teammate? Can the defender cover both of you?
  • Can you win it back immediately? Why are we watching?
Intervene
  • You have a two on one. Where is your teammate? How do you use them?
  • What are you going to do differently on the next rep?
Observe
Cognition
  • Quality and direction of first touch — into space or into trouble?
  • In 2v1: does the player on the ball look for their partner or default to going alone?
  • In 2v1: are attackers reading when and where to commit in the longer box?
  • Eye contact between attacking partners
  • Does the defender read the attacker’s first touch to anticipate direction?
Competence
  • First touch quality under immediate defensive pressure
  • Technical quality of moves and feints in a live 1v1
  • Defensive body position, footwork, and recovery angle
Character
  • Response to losing the 1v1 — resets mentally or carries frustration?
  • Effort to win ball back immediately after losing it
  • Attitude toward partner in 2v1 — encouraging or ignoring?
Evaluation Priority  Silver Gate

The 2v1 is your first direct Silver gate filter. A player who dominates 1v1 but ignores their partner in 2v1 is telling you exactly what you need to know.

Block 5
SSGs Three Format Rotation
3 pools running simultaneously  ·  ~12–13 players + GK per pool  ·  Pool movement at transitions
Format
3 simultaneous pools
Per pool
6v6 + GK
Time
0:50–1:20
Duration
30 min
Three Pool Setup — SSG 1 Center Goal / SSG 2 Outside Goals / SSG 3 Large Goals with Thirds
Pool Rotation Structure
Bubble pool Runs formats in sequence: Format 1 first, Format 2 second, Format 3 third.
Silver + Bronze pools Run formats in operational order. Pool movement between pools happens at each rotation transition — Justin and Jim move players deliberately.
Format 1
Middle Goal Line with Puggs

Run It

  • Puggs placed on the goal line in the middle of the field
  • Standard 5v5 or 6v6, free play
  • Score by passing or dribbling through the puggs
  • No constraints — let them play
Out Loud
  • Can we find the combination?
  • Where is the space? Can you see it before you receive?
  • Are we communicating?
Intervene
  • You have options. What are you seeing?
  • Who is organizing this? Can somebody take ownership?
Observe — Format 1
Cognition
  • Natural tendencies with no constraint — finds teammates, goes alone, organizes, or disappears?
  • Scans before receiving
  • Moves to create options for teammates
Competence
  • Passing weight and accuracy in game context
  • First touch quality under light pressure
  • Technical execution in open play
Character
  • Who leads and organizes without being asked
  • Who communicates consistently vs who goes quiet
  • Response to mistakes in open play
Format 2
Outside Puggs

Run It

  • Puggs placed on the outside edges of the goal line on both sides
  • Standard 5v5 or 6v6
  • Score by passing or dribbling through the wide puggs
  • Attacking team must find wide channels to score
  • Defending team must communicate and hold shape to cover both wide options
Out Loud
  • Can you find the wide option? Where is the space?
  • Defenders — are you covering both sides? Who is communicating the shape?
  • Can you switch it? What does the weak side look like?
Intervene
  • Defenders — who is organizing your shape? Can somebody take ownership of that?
  • You have a wide option. Can you find the angle?
Observe — Format 2
Cognition
  • Who identifies and exploits the wide channel
  • Who switches the point of attack when one side is closed
  • Defensive organization — who communicates shape and covers the weak side?
  • Who keeps forcing the closed option vs who solves the problem quickly?
Competence
  • Crossing and wide delivery quality
  • Defensive positioning and footwork in wide areas
  • Combination play to create the wide angle
Character
  • Defensive communication under pressure — who organizes vs who goes silent?
  • Response when the wide option is closed — adaptability or frustration?
  • Who lifts teammates when the defending team holds their shape well
Format 3
Pop Up Goals — Field Divided into Thirds

Run It

  • Pop up goals placed at each end
  • Field divided into thirds with cones
  • Players must be in their attacking third before a shot counts
  • Standard 6v6
  • Teams must move the ball with purpose through the middle third before attacking
Out Loud
  • Can you move it through the middle? What does the space look like in the final third?
  • Defenders — where are you holding your shape? Are you giving them the middle third for free?
  • When do you go? Is this the right moment to shoot?
Intervene
  • You are rushing it. Can you build through the middle first?
  • You have space in the final third. What are you waiting for?
Observe — Format 3
Cognition
  • Decision making — when to shoot vs when to build?
  • Movement patterns through the middle third — creating space or running into each other?
  • Defensive press decisions — how high, when to drop, who makes the call?
  • Who recognizes the moment to transition from building to attacking
Competence
  • Shot quality and accuracy from medium distance
  • Combination play through the middle third
  • Defensive shape and press execution
Character
  • Patience in possession — who forces early vs who waits for the right moment?
  • Who drives the team forward when possession stalls
  • Defensive effort tracking runners into the final third
Evaluation Priority  Silver Gate Bubble

Format 3 is your highest Cognition demand of the night. A player who moves the ball with purpose, identifies the right moment to attack, and makes accurate decisions about when and where to shoot is showing you Cognition and Competence working together. This is your confirmation read on every bubble player before the night closes.

Block 6
Large Scrimmage
Three-team round robin  ·  Losing team rotates off  ·  No coach interventions — observe only
Format
3-team round robin
Teams
~12–13 per team
Rounds
10 min rounds
Time
1:20–1:45

Run It

  • Three teams of roughly 12–13 players
  • Losing team rotates off, winning team stays on, next team comes on
  • 10-minute rounds
  • No constraints — let it breathe
  • No coach interventions — coaches watch, do not coach
Observe
Cognition
  • Target attention on players with open questions from earlier blocks
  • Sideline team — who is watching vs who is checked out
Competence
  • Confirm reads, do not build new ones
  • Touch count per player is low — moments of quality matter
Character
  • Sideline team behavior — stays engaged and competitive vs mentally leaves
  • Response to losing and rotating off
  • Who self-organizes their team before kickoff
Evaluation Priority  Silver Gate

Open scrimmage is harder to fake than structured activities. Watch for gaps between drill performance and game performance. Leadership in self-organization before kickoff is a Silver indicator.
