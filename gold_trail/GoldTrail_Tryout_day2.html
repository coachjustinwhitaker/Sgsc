Parallel Lines with Center Cones — Pairs Move Simultaneously
Round Structure
Rounds 1–3 No ball. Movement base. Jog, high knees, skips, carioca, lateral shuffle, backpedal to center then sprint out.
Rounds 4–6 Ball introduced. Passing patterns and dribbling decisions. Head up. Firm passes to feet. Both players arrive at the center cone simultaneously.
Rounds 7–9 Technical dribbling. Direction changes, change of pace, inside/outside, cuts. Push the pace. Watch coordination and comfort on the ball at speed.
Session Note

Watch body language from the first minute. Players who are switched on and moving with purpose before the first coaching word are telling you something.

What to Watch For
  • Arrives at the center cone in sync with their partner versus goes at their own pace
  • Competitive posture and effort from the opening minute
  • In the dribbling rounds: coordination at pace, head up or eyes down on the ball
  • Who responds to correction and tries the adjustment

Offset Shoot and Chase

Field
Full width or 40–50 yds

2 goals, offset 15–20 yds

Rotation
Shoot, defend, rest, repeat
Two End-Line Goals, Offset Laterally — Shoot then Immediately Defend

How It Works

Player A starts at Endline 2 with ball, drives toward Goal 1, shoots. Immediately after A shoots, Player B starts at Endline 1 toward Goal 2. Player A must sprint to close down Player B and defend Goal 2. After B shoots, Player C starts and B defends. Continuous cycle. Rotate: shoot, defend, rest, repeat.

Session Note

This is a fitness and mentality filter more than a technical one. Do not spend correction time on finishing mechanics here. Work rate in both directions is the data point.

What to Watch For
  • Sprints back immediately after shooting versus jogs
  • Closes down the defender hard versus goes through the motions
  • Maintains intensity in Round 2 as fatigue builds
  • Who is still competing when tired and who is managing their effort

Full-Size Goal, Set GK — Server Wide at Midfield, Defender 5 Yards Behind

How It Works

Server plays ball into the attacker. Defender starts their recovery run simultaneously from 5 yards behind. Attacker must decide: shoot early before the defender closes, or take on the GK. Natural rest is built into the rotation. Run both sides simultaneously if numbers allow. Rotate the server, attacker, and defender roles. GK stays in goal throughout.

Session Note

Decision timing is the evaluation point. Watch who reads the situation early and commits. The defender role shows you sprint effort and recovery attitude just as clearly as the attacker role.

What to Watch For
  • Reads keeper position and defender distance before deciding, or just reacts late
  • Commits to the finish with a clear decision versus hesitates and loses the window
  • Places the finish versus blasts it
  • When corrected on body shape or timing, tries the adjustment on the next rep
  • Defender: full sprint to recover or managed effort

Cascading Waves — Pair Attacks, Then Becomes Defenders Against the Next Pair

How It Works

Pair 1 attacks 2v1 versus the GK. As soon as Pair 1 shoots, Pair 2 enters and Pair 1 now defends. Repeats in constant cascading waves. Pause after 5 minutes, ask what is working briefly, then repeat the second rep. Mix pairings intentionally.

Session Note

Fatigue is starting to set in. Pair borderline players with stronger players and watch how they respond. Pair two borderline players together and watch who leads. The combinations reveal things the individual activities do not.

What to Watch For
  • Communicates with their partner and combines, or goes alone
  • Attacking pair becomes a defending unit immediately after shooting, or stands and watches
  • Who makes the third-man run and who stands flat
  • Who leads when paired with a weaker player
  • Mental sharpness and communication quality as fatigue builds

Free Game — Full Field, Normal Rules

Floater Role

Borderline players start as floaters, available to either team in possession. Watch who demands the ball constantly and who waits to be found.

Observe Only

Let it run without instruction. An hour of structured work has already run. What players do now without coaching is what they actually are.

What to Watch For
  • Who organizes teammates when tired, without being asked
  • Who communicates and who goes quiet under fatigue
  • Who demands the ball in the floater role versus who waits
  • Habits that appear without coaching: pressing, defensive shape, transition decisions
  • Who is still competing in the final 5 minutes
