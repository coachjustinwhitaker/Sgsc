Activity 1  ·  Warm Up
Arrival SSG
3v3 or 4v4  ·  Pugg goals  ·  Start as soon as enough players arrive  ·  10 min
Format
3v3 or 4v4
Goals
Pugg goals each end
Rule
Full press on turnover
Coaching
None — just reps
Small Field — Pugg Goals Each End, Swap Teams as Players Arrive

Setup

Go as soon as enough players arrive. Swap teams as more show up. Keep it moving immediately.

The Only Rule

Full press — when you lose the ball, win it back immediately. No standing around.

Watch For

No coaching during this block. Just reps and energy. Watch who competes from the first minute and who needs to warm up mentally.

Activity 2  ·  Warm Up
Mirrored Dynamic Warm-Up
Parallel lines with center cones  ·  Pairs move simultaneously  ·  20 min
Setup
Parallel lines, center cones
Groups
Pairs, 2 per line
Active/Rest
30s on / 15s per pair
Duration
20 min
Parallel Lines — Both Players Move Simultaneously to Center and Across
  • High knees — drive the knees up, arms pumping
  • Butt kicks — heels to glutes, stay on your toes
  • Lateral shuffle both directions — stay low, do not cross your feet
  • Hip openers — open the gate, close the gate, alternate legs at each step
  • Lunge with rotation — long step, twist toward the front knee, keep chest tall
  • Carioca — hips rotating, stay light on your feet
  • Dribble — ball introduced, head up, move with pace
  • Pass — firm passes to feet, keep both players arriving together
Coaching Cues
  • "Stay on your toes — flat feet slow you down"
  • "Last two runs — accelerate through the finish line, do not slow down before it"
Activity 3  ·  Possession
Rondo
4v1 or 5v2  ·  Quick decisions and clean first touch  ·  10 min
Format
4v1 or 5v2
Grid (4v1)
~10×10 yards
Grid (5v2)
~12×12 yards
Touch limit
1–2 touches
Tight Grid — Defender in Middle, Possession Players on Outside

Rules

Defender in the middle wins the ball or forces it out. Player who loses the ball or gives a bad pass goes in the middle. Standard rondo rules. Keep grids tight and defenders honest — if the outside players are too comfortable the grid is too big.

Coaching Points
  • "Receive with an open body — can you see the whole grid before the ball arrives?"
  • "What is your next pass before you receive?"
  • "One or two touches maximum — keep it moving"
  • "If you are in the middle, where is the ball going next? Read it before it gets there"
Activity 4  ·  Technical
Passing and Shooting Pattern
Striker layoff, CM through ball, winger finish  ·  Both sides simultaneously  ·  15 min
Roles
Striker, CM, Winger
Goal
Full-size + GK
Lines
Both sides, natural stagger
Duration
15 min
Central Pattern — Striker Layoff, CM Through Ball, Winger Finish
Pattern Sequence
1Striker checks back to receive from the CM line
2Striker lays off one touch to the CM
3CM plays a through ball into the channel for the overlapping winger who is already running
4Winger receives in stride and finishes

Key Timing Points

Striker check must happen before the CM plays the ball. Winger run starts as the CM receives the layoff — not after. CM through ball must be weighted to arrive at the winger's run, not behind them.

Progressions
Phase 1No defenders. Build the pattern at three-quarter pace. Get the timing right before adding speed.
Phase 2Add a passive defender shadowing the striker to make the layoff more realistic.
Phase 3Full pace, both sides running simultaneously. GK active.
Coach Note

This is the 2-3-1 in action — this is how we attack. When the timing clicks it will feel like a real game moment. Name it when it happens.

Coaching Points
  • "Striker — check away first, then check back. Create separation before you receive."
  • "CM — one touch layoff, head up, play it into the space ahead of the runner"
  • "Winger — start your run as the CM receives. Do not wait to see where the ball goes."
  • "Through ball weight — too hard and it runs away, too soft and the defender gets it"
  • "Finish — body set, place it, do not blast"
Activity 5  ·  Technical
Defending: Delay and Recovery
1v1 delay, recovering defender joins, builds to 2v2  ·  15×20 yard grids  ·  15 min
Grid
15×20 yards
Start
1v1 then recover
Recovery cone
10 yds behind D1
Duration
15 min
15×20 Grid — First Defender Delays, Second Defender Sprints from Cone to Support

How It Works

Attacker starts with ball and tries to dribble to the far end line. First defender delays — goal-side, force a direction, stay on feet. Recovering defender sprints from the cone to get goal-side and support. Once the recovering defender arrives both defenders work together. Run multiple grids simultaneously.

Progressions
Phase 11v1 delay only. No recovering defender. Focus purely on body position, distance, and showing one direction.
Phase 2Add recovering defender from 10 yards back. First defender must delay long enough for help to arrive.
Phase 32v2. Two attackers combine against two defenders. Defenders must communicate and shift together.
Key Habit

The recovery run is the key habit. Sprint to goal-side first — not to the ball. If the recovering defender runs to the ball both defenders end up on the same side and the attacker plays around them. Goal-side first, then read the situation.

Delay — First Defender
  • "Show them outside — cut off the middle, force them wide"
  • "Stay on your feet — do not dive in unless you are certain"
  • "Slow them down — buy time for your teammate"
  • "Close enough to pressure, far enough not to get beaten"
Recovery — Second Defender
  • "Sprint to goal-side, not to the ball — get between them and the goal first"
  • "Communicate — let your partner know you are there"
  • "Once you arrive — who has ball, who has cover?"
Two Defenders Together
  • "First defender — once help arrives you can be braver. Step when they take a heavy touch."
  • "Two defenders form a wall, not a line — one pressures, one covers the space behind"
Activity 6  ·  Scrimmage
Positional Scrimmage
Assigned positions matching formation  ·  Normal rules  ·  Brief freezes only  ·  18 min
Format
Positional, full formation
Rules
Normal soccer
Freezes
Short, one point, restart fast
Duration
18 min
Positional Scrimmage — Assigned Formation, Normal Rules

Focus

Through ball moments and defending transitions. Watch for: timing of runs beyond the defensive line, weight of through passes, first defender stepping immediately when possession is lost, recovery runs getting goal-side after turnovers.

Freeze Triggers
Missed opportunity A through ball opportunity that was missed — freeze, show the run, restart.
Defensive lapse A turnover where the first defender does not step immediately — freeze, reset the defensive shape, restart.
Great moment Something goes really well — freeze, name it, celebrate it, restart.
Coach Note

End on a goal or a positive moment if you can. The last thing they remember from training sticks. If the scrimmage is flowing well do not freeze it — let them finish on a high.

Coaching During Freezes
  • Keep freezes under 30 seconds — the game is the teacher
  • Ask questions rather than telling: "What did you see there?" "Where could the pass have gone?"
  • Let them play. Your job in the scrimmage is to observe and ask, not to direct.
Close
Bring Them In

"What is one thing you are taking into the next game?"

Let every player answer if time allows. Keep it fast and positive. End with energy.
