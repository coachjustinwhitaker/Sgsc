Philosophy Connection

The wall pass taught players that a teammate can solve their problem. Today takes that idea into the wide channel. The winger drives at a defender, the fullback reads the moment and goes with them, calls "with you," and forces a decision. Support is not passive. It is arriving at the right moment, announcing yourself, and making the ball carrier's choice easier. That is what this session builds toward.

Session Objectives
  • Winger drives at the defender with purpose to commit them
  • Fullback reads the drive and goes with them at the right moment
  • Fullback calls "with you" so the winger knows the option is there
  • Winger makes a decision based on what they hear and see, dribble or release
  • After the combination, both players continue into the next action

Activity Diagram
ObjectiveInstall the winger-fullback overlap. Winger drives to commit the defender. Fullback goes with them, calls "with you," and forces the decision. Winger reads what they hear and see: dribble or release.
Sets
Set 1 · Winger OnlyWinger drives around the cone and at the opposing fullback. Beats them 1v1 and drives to the end zone. Install the drive habit before adding the overlap.
Set 2 · Winger + FullbackWinger drives. Fullback follows and calls "with you." Winger decides: dribble or release. If released, fullback controls and drives into the end zone. Winger falls into support.
Coach Note

Set 1 is not a throwaway. The winger needs to believe they can beat the defender before they will commit to the drive in Set 2. If the drive is tentative, go back to Set 1 for more reps. The overlap only works if the defender is actually committed to the winger.

Coaching Points
  • Winger: drive at pace, make the defender commit
  • Fullback: go with the winger, call "with you" before they need you
  • Winger: listen for the call, then decide
  • After the release: both players continue, nobody stops to watch

ObjectiveAdd the 10 cutting into the half space. Now the winger has two options: overlap with the fullback or release inside to the 10. The defender has to choose which threat to track. Set 2 adds the 5 to the defensive shape, making the combination sharper to execute.
Sets
Set 1 · 3v2Winger, fullback, and 10 attack. Opposing fullback and recovering 8 defend. Winger drives, fullback calls "with you," 10 cuts inside to the half space. Winger reads: overlap, inside pass, or dribble. Attack plays to the pugg representing the 9. Defense plays to the central pugg if they win it.
Set 2 · 3v3Same attacking trio. 5 steps into the defensive shape alongside the fullback and 8. The combination now has to be sharper because the defense is more organized. Same scoring targets.
Coach Note

The 10's cut inside is the new element. If players are only using the overlap and ignoring the inside option, freeze play and ask: what did you see that told you the overlap was the right choice? The decision is the point, not the specific combination.

Coaching Points
  • Winger: you now have two options, read which defender commits
  • 10: time the cut inside so you arrive when the ball is ready, not before
  • Fullback: still call "with you," your run keeps the overlap threat alive even if the ball goes inside
  • Set 2: the combination has to be faster and cleaner, the defense is organized now

Vocabulary This Session
With You
The call a supporting player makes so the ball carrier knows they have an option. Forces the defender to choose between two threats. No call, no decision.
When: any supporting player arrives in a useful position behind or beyond the ball carrier
Overlap
Supporting player runs beyond the ball carrier in the wide channel. Creates a 2v1 by taking a defender in a second direction.
When: fullback runs beyond the winger to create width and depth simultaneously

Question 1
When the fullback called "with you," what did that actually change for the winger?
Listen forPlayers who name a specific effect: the defender had to choose, a lane opened, the decision became easier. That reasoning connects the support action to the outcome. "It helped" without naming how is close but not there yet.

Is there anyone you want to recognize today?
