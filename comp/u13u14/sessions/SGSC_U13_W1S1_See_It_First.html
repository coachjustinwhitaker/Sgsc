Philosophy Connection

First session of the fall. Before anything else gets built, this is where it starts: what you see before the ball arrives. Every block we run this season depends on this habit, so today is not about results, it is about installing the picture: scan before you receive, open your body to the field, and let your first touch be a decision instead of a reaction. Get comfortable being uncomfortable early. That is what today is for.

Session Objectives
  • Scan before receiving to identify pressure and open space on every touch, not only when reminded
  • Begin orienting the body to see the field before the ball arrives, not after it does
  • Recognize the obvious difference between having time on the ball and a forced one-touch situation
  • Treat first touch as a decision, not just control, and redirect into space with purpose
  • Use small, controlled touches to set up the next action under introductory pressure

40×40 Grid — 4 Puggs at Each Side Midpoint
Objective Get bodies moving while immediately demanding the habit we are installing today: scan before every touch. All four teams live at once, not fixed pairs, so the picture never settles into a single matchup. Sets the tone from the first whistle, eyes up before the ball arrives.

Scoring & Rules

All four teams play simultaneously: A v B v C v D. Each team defends the pugg nearest them and can attack any of the other three. No fixed attacker/defender roles.

Restarts

Two balls active at once. If a ball goes out, a new one comes in immediately from the sideline. Keep it moving, do not let it die.

Coaching Points
  • Before you receive, which goal is open right now, and which one just closed?
  • Are you scanning before the ball gets to you, or after?
  • First session of the year. Over-communicate with each other, call out space
  • Celebrate a good scan even if the shot misses: “You saw that early. That is exactly right.”

Technical Stations — Format

17 players split into 3 groups, one coach per station. Each group rotates through all three stations below, roughly 9–11 minutes per rotation, about 27–33 minutes total.

Speed Box — Player A In, Player B Tosses From Outside
Objective Build a wide base of first-touch surfaces under a toss that arrives on its own timing, not a predictable service. No scanning demand here by design. Stations 2 and 3 own that. This station owns pure technique and reaction.

Pattern

Player A works quick feet in the speed box. The instant A steps out, Player B tosses the ball in for the return: laces, inside foot, knee-to-foot pass (bring the ball down), chest to foot pass, shoulder pass back (L), shoulder pass back (R), headers. 2 sets each.

Finish & Switch

After the technique series, A sprints 15 yards out and jogs back. A and B fully switch roles and repeat.

Coach Note

U13 heading guidance caps players at roughly 30 minutes of heading work per week. Track headers here against anything else heading-related this week so it doesn't stack up unnoticed.

Coaching Points
  • Player A: quick feet in the box, explosive step out, not a lazy shuffle
  • Clean first touch before the return. Are you rushing the technique to get to the sprint?
  • Player B: vary the toss slightly, do not serve the same spot every rep
  • Full effort on the 15-yard sprint. This is a touches station, not a rest station

L-Shape — Pivot Receives Back Foot, Scans, Turns, Passes Other Foot
Objective Isolate today's habit in its cleanest form: scan the next target while the ball is still traveling, receive across the body to open up, then play out with the other foot. Phase 1 is a clean picture, no pressure. Phase 2 adds a driving progression on top of the same read.
Progressions
Phase 1 · Base PatternBall travels down one line into the pivot player at the corner of the L. Pivot receives on the back foot, the foot farthest from the pass, scanning the next target before the ball arrives, then turns and plays into the second line with the other foot. Rotate the pivot spot every 3–4 reps so everyone gets it.
Phase 2 · Driving ProgressionReceive and push the ball into space with the outside of the foot, drive to the next player, leave the ball for them, then backpedal back to center. That next player repeats the same pattern to a third player, continuing the chain.
Coaching Points
  • Is the scan happening while the ball is traveling, or after you have already controlled it?
  • Back foot on the receive. Is your body already opening toward the next target?
  • Phase 2: is the outside-foot touch actually sending you into space, or just sideways?
  • Backpedal back to center, eyes on play, not a turn-and-jog
  • Pivot rotates on the whistle. Everyone gets this rep today
Activity 5 of 6  ·  Technical Station 3 of 3
Station 3 — Find the Inside Player
Rondo With a Mandatory Inside Option  ·  9–11 minutes
Format
Perimeter + inside target
Pressure
1–2 defenders
Rule
Play inside player if open
Duration
9–11 min
Rondo Grid — Inside Player Live, Must Be Found When Open
Objective Apply the scan under real pressure. Same read as Station 2: is the inside option open? Now a defender can close that lane, so the picture is live instead of clean. This is the pressure version of the same habit.

Rule

Perimeter players keep possession from 1–2 defenders inside the grid, plus a live inside target player who can also be marked. If the inside player is open, the next pass must find him. If a defender is blocking the lane, circulate outside instead.

Enforcement

Station coach makes the call. Missing an open inside player is a turnover to the defenders. Keeps it self-officiating and keeps the coach coaching instead of managing a card.

Coaching Points
  • Before your next touch: is the inside player open right now?
  • Defenders: can you see when you are being used to close that lane, not just chasing the ball?
  • Inside player: are you making yourself easy to find, or hiding?
  • This is the same read as Station 2. Now someone is trying to stop you from making it
Activity 6 of 6  ·  Block Training
See It, Play It
9v9  ·  Set 1 Touch-Restricted, Set 2 Free Play  ·  14–16 minutes
Format
9v9
Field
Full field
Sets
2, ~7–8 min each
Duration
14–16 min
Full Field — Two Goals, GK Both Ends, 9v9
Objective Put everything from today into a live picture. Set 1's three-touch limit removes the option to hide, rewarding the players who already scanned before the ball arrives. Set 2 drops the restriction entirely: the real test is whether the habit holds up once nothing is forcing it. (Standard tightens to 2-touch when B1 returns at Development.)
Structure
Set 1 · ~7–8 min · 3-Touch MaxTouch limit on. Rewards the early scan and the quick decision.
Set 2 · ~7–8 min · No RestrictionsFree play. Watch whether the scanning habit holds up once the training wheel is off.
Coach Note

This is a discovery game, not a lecture. Let Set 1's touch limit do the teaching: players who scanned early will look calm, players who did not will panic on the ball. Freeze it briefly if needed to ask what they saw, then restart fast.

Coaching Points
  • Before you receive: where is your outlet if the first read is wrong?
  • What told you to play that pass: something you saw, or a guess?
  • Set 2: with no touch limit, is the habit still there, or does it disappear the moment the constraint is gone?
  • Celebrate the clean sequence out loud. That is today's habit showing up live

Question 1

Listen forPressure and space, where the defender is, where the open option is. If they cannot name it specifically, point back to the stations and See It, Play It.

Listen forPanic, heavy touch, lost possession. Let them name the discomfort. That is exactly what today was designed to create.
Question 3
What is the difference between controlling the ball and making a decision with your first touch?
Listen forControl is just stopping it. A decision moves it somewhere on purpose. Tie back to Station 2’s receive-scan-turn.

Who scanned early today and made it look easy? Call them out.
Listen forLet teammates recognize each other. Build the culture that scanning is the standard, not a bonus.
